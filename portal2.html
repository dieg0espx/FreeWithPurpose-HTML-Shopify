What to Watch Out For (Because "Clean" Isn't Regulated)

  • No universal regulation: "Clean beauty" has no official regulatory definition that ensures what a brand calls "clean" meets the same standards globally.
  • Transparency varies: Some brands are very transparent, others less so. Even among "clean" brands you often need to read individual product ingredient lists, not just the general brand "story."
  • Natural ≠ harmless: "Natural" or "botanical" ingredients can still cause irritation, allergies, or photosensitivity in some people. Clean beauty aims to avoid toxins — but natural extracts come with their own risks.
  • Sustainability & ethics often unclear / partial: A brand may avoid certain chemicals, but that doesn't necessarily mean sustainable packaging, ethical sourcing, or low-waste manufacturing — these require separate commitments.
